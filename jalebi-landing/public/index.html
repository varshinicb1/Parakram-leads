why the name

It's named after a sweet that shouldn't work — but does.

A jalebi starts as loose, shapeless batter. It only becomes something worth eating once it's coiled — piped into tight, tangled spirals, fried until crisp, then soaked all the way through in syrup. Crispy on the outside. Soft, sweet, and dense with flavor at the core.

That's exactly what this installer does to your laptop. It takes a plain, idle Windows machine and coils real infrastructure around it — an SSH server, an encrypted tunnel, a service that restarts itself, a dashboard that tells the truth — until what's left is small, dense, and quietly excellent. Crispy security on the outside. A sweet, simple experience at the core.

Also — nobody in the world had used it for a VPS product yet. Now we have.
